歳

Asking & telling your age

There are two ways to ask. One is plain, one is extra polite. The answer for both is the same: a number + さい sai.

なんさい ですか。nan sai desu ka.How old are you? — plain (なん “what” + さい “years old”)
おいくつ ですか。o-ikutsu desu ka.How old are you? — polite / softer
The counter for age is さい (歳). Attach it to a number, then end with です.

Answering

わたしは     さい です。

watashi wa ___ sai desu. — “I am ___ years old.”

You can drop わたしは once it's obvious you're talking about yourself — ___ さいです is enough.

数

Numbers + さい

Mostly it's just the plain number + さい. A few harden the sound — 1, 8, 10 turn into a small つ + さい, and 20 has its own special word.

#JapaneseNotes
1いっさいissaigotchaっ before さい (not “ichisai”).
2にさいni sai
3さんさいsan sai
4よんさいyon sai
5ごさいgo sai
6ろくさいroku sai
7ななさいnana sai
8はっさいhassaigotchaっ before さい (not “hachisai”).
9きゅうさいkyuu sai
10じゅっさい・じっさいjussai / jissaigotchaBoth readings are fine; じゅっさい is more common.
11じゅういっさいjuu issai10 + 1 → keeps the っ from いっさい.
20はたちhatachispecialはたち — totally irregular, not “nijuu sai”.

Bigger numbers — the tens

For 30, 40 and up you just build the number normally, then add さい (e.g. sanjuu sai = 30 years old).

30さんじゅうsanjuu→ さんじゅっさい sanjussai = 30 yrs
40よんじゅうyonjuu→ よんじゅっさい yonjussai = 40 yrs
Lock in the irregulars: 1 = issai, 8 = hassai, 10 = jussai, and 20 = hatachi. Everything else is plain number + さい.
月

Months — number + がつ

Months are dead simple: the month number + がつ gatsu (月). “January” is literally “1-month”, “February” is “2-month”, and so on.

#JapaneseEnglish
1月いちがつichi gatsuJanuary
2月にがつni gatsuFebruary
3月さんがつsan gatsuMarch
4月しがつshi gatsugotchaApril — し, not “yon”.
5月ごがつgo gatsuMay
6月ろくがつroku gatsuJune
7月しちがつshichi gatsugotchaJuly — しち, not “nana”.
8月はちがつhachi gatsuAugust
9月くがつku gatsugotchaSeptember — く, not “kyuu”.
10月じゅうがつjuu gatsuOctober
11月じゅういちがつjuuichi gatsuNovember
12月じゅうにがつjuuni gatsuDecember
The three to watch: 4月 = しがつ (shi), 7月 = しちがつ (shichi), 9月 = くがつ (ku) — same odd readings as in the hours.
日

Days of the month — the date

This is the trickiest set. Days 1–10 (plus 14, 20, 24) use old native readings you have to memorise. From 11 on it's mostly regular: number + にち nichi (日).

曜

Days of the week

Every day of the week ends in ようび youbi (曜日). The first part is an element — moon, fire, water, wood, metal, earth, sun.

JapaneseEnglishFrom
げつようびgetsu youbiMonday月 moon
かようびka youbiTuesday火 fire
すいようびsui youbiWednesday水 water
もくようびmoku youbiThursday木 wood
きんようびkin youbiFriday金 metal
どようびdo youbiSaturday土 earth
にちようびnichi youbiSunday日 sun

Which day is it today?

きょうは なんようび ですか。

kyou wa nan youbi desu ka. — “What day of the week is it today?”

きょうtoday はtopic なんwhat ようびday of week ですis か?
なん “what” swaps in for the day; か at the end is the spoken question mark.

Answering

きょうは     ようび です。

kyou wa ___ youbi desu. — “Today is ___day.”
きょうは げつようび です。kyou wa getsu youbi desu.Today is Monday.
きょうは どようび です。kyou wa do youbi desu.Today is Saturday.
