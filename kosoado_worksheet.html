The lesson

Every word in this family starts with one of four sounds. The starting sound tells you distance from the speaker — or, for do-, that you're asking.

Where each thing sits

You are the Speaker. The produce below is placed at the three distances. The do- words are what you say when you don't know which/where.

Distance is measured from you, the speaker — not from the page.

Two things to watch: the place word for "far" is asoko (not "ako") — the one irregular cell. And a + noun word can never stand alone: it's kono ringo, never just "kono."

Asking questions

"What" is its own word: なに / なん nani / nan (use nan before desu). Here are the four patterns your slides showed:

これは なんですか。kore wa nan desu ka.What is this?
どれですか。dore desu ka.Which one is it?
どの りんごですか。dono ringo desu ka.Which apple is it?
どこですか。doko desu ka.Where is it?

Pattern to remember: [topic] wa [question word] desu ka. The little か ka at the end is the spoken question mark.

A · demonstratives

Translate the phrase. Mind both the distance and the row.

  1. this apple + noun · in your hand
  2. that tomato + noun · by the listener
  3. that grape over there + noun · far away
  4. this pronoun · the apple
  5. that one over there pronoun · the far thing
  6. here place · where you are
  7. there place · where the listener is
  8. over there place · far from both

D · full sentences

Build the whole sentence. Everything from the lesson is fair game.

  1. This is an apple.
  2. That (by you) is a tomato.
  3. That over there is a grape.
  4. What is that (by the listener)?
  5. Which potato is it?
  6. Where is the cucumber? cucumber wa … ?
E · at the market

Picture it: you're holding a strawberry. The seller (the listener) has a carrot. A banana stand sits far across the market. Say each line.

  1. Point at the strawberry in your hand: "this strawberry."
  2. Point at the seller's carrot: "that carrot."
  3. Nod toward the far stand: "that banana (over there)."
  4. Ask the seller, holding your strawberry: "What is this?"
  5. Ask where the banana stand is: "Where is it?"
