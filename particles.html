Quick particle map

Particles come after a word and show what job that word is doing. This page keeps the older particles visible, then adds new sections for も mo, と to, and が ga.

も

New section: も means “also” or “too”

も is pronounced mo. Put it right after the noun or phrase you want to mark as “also.”

Noun + も

noun + mo — “also / too”

私わたしもアメリカ人じんです。

watashi mo amerika-jin desu. — “I am American too.”

私わたしI もalso / too アメリカ人じんAmerican ですam / is
The hiragana above the kanji shows how to read it: 私 = わたし, 人 = じん.

How it changes a sentence

Without もWith も
私わたしは アメリカ人じんです。watashi wa amerika-jin desu.I am American. 私わたしもアメリカ人じんです。watashi mo amerika-jin desu.I am American too.
Shortcut: when you mean “I too...” or “me too,” わたしも is usually the piece you need.

More examples

私わたしも行いきたいです！watashi mo ikitai desu!I want to go too!
テレビも買かいました。terebi mo kaimashita.I bought a TV too. / I also bought a TV.
と

New section: と connects nouns

と is pronounced to. It means “and” between nouns, and can also mean “with” when you do something together with someone.

Noun + と + noun

noun + to + noun — “noun and noun”
Rule: put と after each noun except the last one.

“And” examples

アイスクリームとかき氷ごおりが好すきです。aisu kuriimu to kakigoori ga suki desu.I like ice cream and shaved ice.
コーヒーとチーズケーキとお茶ちゃをください。koohii to chiizu keeki to ocha o kudasai.Coffee, cheesecake, and tea, please.
ワンピースとTシャツを買かいました。wan piisu to tii shatsu o kaimashita.I bought One Piece and a T-shirt.

Together / with someone

友達ともだちと映画えいがを見みました。

tomodachi to eiga o mimashita. — “I watched a movie with my friend.”

友達ともだちfriend とwith 映画えいがmovie をobject 見みましたwatched
が

New section: が marks the subject

が is pronounced ga. In this lesson, use it to point to what exists: the thing or person that “is there.”

Noun + が + あります / います

noun + ga + arimasu / imasu — “there is / there are...”
EndingUse it for
ありますarimasuthings, objects, places
いますimasupeople and animals

People and animals: が います

妹いもうとがいます。imouto ga imasu.I have a younger sister. / There is a younger sister.
友達ともだちがいます。tomodachi ga imasu.I have a friend. / There is a friend.
犬いぬがいます。inu ga imasu.I have a dog. / There is a dog.
Existence shortcut: if the noun is alive, use が います. If it is not alive, use が あります.
